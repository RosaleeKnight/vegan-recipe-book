Plant based dishes with no animal byproducts and drinks for all occassions.

Ingredients

---Crispy Cauliflower---

1 head of cauliflower, broken into florets [around 650-700g]

3/4 cup panko breadcrumbs [45g]

6 tbsp (about 1/3 cup) fine breadcrumbs [60g]

1/2 cup chickpea flour [60g]

3/4 cup water

---Sauce---

3 tbsp soy sauce, low sodium [45ml]

1 tbsp rice vinegar [15 ml]

1/4 cup grade A maple syrup [60ml]

1 tbsp sesame oil [15ml]

1/2 tsp ground ginger

1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (or to taste)

1 cup water

---Cornstarch slurry---

2 tbsp cornstarch

2 tbsp water

---Toppings---

2-3 stalks of green onion, chopped

Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 425F (218C). Cut the cauliflower in half and cut the green bottom off.

2. Break the cauliflower into medium sized florets. Then cut the stems off. The florets should all be around the same size.

3. Prepare chickpea flour “egg” wash by adding chickpea flour and water into a bowl and mix together until smooth.

4. Add the breadcrumbs into a big bowl.

5. Using one hand add a floret into the chickpea flour “egg” wash and drip off excess.

6. Then using the other hand coat the floret in the breadcrumbs. I also like to sometimes shake the bowl to coat it. Using a wet hand and dry hand will help make the process more efficient.

7. Place the cauliflower on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

8. Once they are all on baking sheet place in the oven for 20-25 minutes. They will be golden and look dry when they are done.

9. While they cook prepare the sauce. First, make the cornstarch slurry by adding the water and cornstarch to a small bowl and whisking together.

10. Then in a big bowl or measuring add all of the sauce ingredients (not the slurry yet). Whisk together.

11. Once it is whisked add the slurry in and whisk again.

12. Heat the sauce up on a low heat in a pot on the stove. It should start out light brown and milky looking and turn dark and slightly more transparent. Stirring it every so often for about 5-10 minutes.

13. Once the cauliflower is done cooking let it cool for 15 minutes before tossing in the sauce.

The meal contains ingredients derived from plants, including fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, oils, whole grains, legumes, and beans. It does not contain animal by products animal products, including red meat, poultry, fish, eggs, honey and dairy products such as whey.

All recipes that show multiple items are meals that make 2 servings and single item recipes including drinks create 1 serving.

It's based off of two things, how filling the item is and how long it takes to prepare. Lunch contains moderately filling items that take less then 2hrs to prepare. Dinner contains very filling items which are protien heavy and dishes that take 2hrs or more to prepare (ex: holiday dishes).
