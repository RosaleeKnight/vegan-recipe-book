All Vegan Recipe Book

Plant based dishes with no animal byproducts and drinks for all occassions.

Ingredients

---Crepes---

1 tbsp Oil to fry

200 g flour

200 ml vegan milk

200 ml sparkling water

1 pinch of salt

1 Pinch baking soda

---Spinach Feta filling---

1 tbsp oil to fry

1 garlic clove peeled and minced

1 onion peeled and minced

350 g frozen spinach

200 g vegan cream cheese

150 g vegan feta substitute or tofu crumbled

Splash of lemon juice

Salt and pepper to taste

---Toppings---

Vegan feta substitute or tofu

Pine nuts

Instructions

---Crepes---

1. Preheat oven to 180C/360F and lightly grease your oven dish.

2. Put all ingredients for the crepes in a bowl, stir into a smooth dough and let it rest for about 10 minutes.

3. Heat a little oil in a frying pan and use a ladle to add dough to the pan until the bottom of the pan is completely covered and fry on both sides over medium heat.

4. Remove the crepes from the pan and set aside.

---Spinach Feta Filling---

1. Heat oil in a pan.

2. Fry onion and garlic for about 3 minutes.

3. Add frozen spinach and fry with the lid closed and low heat until the spinach is completely thawed.

4. Add cream cheese and feta substitute, mix everything together and season with spices and lemon juice.

5. Put 1-2 tbsp of the filling in the middle of the crepes, roll up and place Distribute 1-2 tbsp each of the mass centered on the pancakes and place in the oven dish.

6. Sprinkle with crumbled feta substitute and pine nuts and bake in the oven for about 10 minutes until golden yellow.

The meal contains ingredients derived from plants, including fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, oils, whole grains, legumes, and beans. It does not contain animal by products animal products, including red meat, poultry, fish, eggs, honey and dairy products such as whey.

All recipes that show multiple items are meals that make 2 servings and single item recipes including drinks create 1 serving.

It's based off of two things, how filling the item is and how long it takes to prepare. Lunch contains moderately filling items that take less then 2hrs to prepare. Dinner contains very filling items which are protien heavy and dishes that take 2hrs or more to prepare (ex: holiday dishes).
