All Vegan Recipe Book

Plant based dishes with no animal byproducts and drinks for all occassions.

Ingredients

---For the Dough---

¾ cup + 1 tbsp warm water

2 cups all-purpose flour, plus more or dusting

1 tsp canola oil or other neutral oil

½ tsp salt

---For the Filling---

100g dry vermicelli noodles, makes 2 cups rehydrated noodles

2 cups (100g) chopped chives

1 medium shredded carrot (makes 1 cup)

1 cup (150g) sliced shiitake mushrooms

1 tbsp canola oil or other neutral oil

1 ½ tsp salt, or more to taste

1-2 tbsp soy sauce, or more to taste

1 tbsp sesame seeds

½ tbsp sesame oil

---For Cooking---

12 tbsp water, divided into 4 batches of 3 tbsp each

4 tbsp oil, divided into 4 batches of 1 tbsp each

Instructions

---For the Dough---

1. Add in 2 cups of flour into a mixing bowl. Create a well in the center of the bowl and pour in the warm water.

2. Use a wooden spoon or spatula to mix the flour and water.

3. Once the dough starts to come together, add in 1 tsp of oil.

4. Use your hands to mix the bits of dough.

5. Knead together for several minutes. If the dough is a bit dry, add a splash of water.⠀

6. Shape the dough into a ball, then place it in a bowl.

7. Cover it with a damp towel and let it rest for 30 minutes.

---For the Filling---

1. Chop the noodles into small ½ inch pieces. Drain excess water and set aside.

2. Heat a pan. Add in 1 tbsp canola or other neutral oil.

3. When the oil is hot, add in the chives, carrots, and mushrooms.

4. Cook over medium high heat for 4-5 minutes until the carrots are tender.

5. Add in noodles. Pour in the soy sauce, salt, sesame seeds and oil.

6. Mix well with a wooden spoon or spatula and cook for another 2-3 minutes over medium heat.

7. Transfer to a strainer to drain excess liquid from the vegetables then set aside to cool while you prepare the wrappers.

Frequently Asked Questions

The meal contains ingredients derived from plants, including fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, oils, whole grains, legumes, and beans. It does not contain animal by products animal products, including red meat, poultry, fish, eggs, honey and dairy products such as whey.

All recipes that show multiple items are meals that make 2 servings and single item recipes including drinks create 1 serving.

It's based off of two things, how filling the item is and how long it takes to prepare. Lunch contains moderately filling items that take less then 2hrs to prepare. Dinner contains very filling items which are protien heavy and dishes that take 2hrs or more to prepare (ex: holiday dishes).
