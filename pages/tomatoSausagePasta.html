All Vegan Recipe Book

Plant based dishes with no animal byproducts and drinks for all occassions.

Ingredients

8oz pasta

4 vegan Italian sausages, sliced

2 tablespoons olive oil

12oz cherry tomatoes (around 1.5 cups)

1/4 tsp salt

1/4 tsp pepper

1/4 tsp red pepper flakes

2 garlic cloves, minced

1/4 cup dry white wine

2 tablespoons tomato paste

5 oz spinach

1 tablespoon vegan butter

1/2 cup reserved pasta water

Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook your pasta according to package instructions.

2. Drain and reserve 1/2 cup of cooking water.

3. Slice your cherry tomatoes, mince your garlic and set aside.

4. Slice sausages and cook according to package instructions.

5. Drain excess oil safety and wipe out the pan. Return the pan back to the heat.

6. With the same pan on medium heat, add your olive oil, cherry tomatoes, red pepper flakes, salt and pepper. Cook tomatoes for 3-5 minutes, smashing the tomatoes with the back of a wooden spoon once softened to release it's juices.

7. Pour in the white wine and minced garlic. Let white wine reduce by half and add in the tomato paste.

8. Mix everything, incorporating the tomato paste into the sauce.

9. Add in your spinach and season with a bit more salt and pepper. Let spinach wilt down.

10. Add in your cooked pasta and sausages. Mix to combine and gradually add in pasta water to thicken the sauce.

11. Add in a tablespoon of butter and mix until melted.

The meal contains ingredients derived from plants, including fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, oils, whole grains, legumes, and beans. It does not contain animal by products animal products, including red meat, poultry, fish, eggs, honey and dairy products such as whey.

All recipes that show multiple items are meals that make 2 servings and single item recipes including drinks create 1 serving.

It's based off of two things, how filling the item is and how long it takes to prepare. Lunch contains moderately filling items that take less then 2hrs to prepare. Dinner contains very filling items which are protien heavy and dishes that take 2hrs or more to prepare (ex: holiday dishes).
