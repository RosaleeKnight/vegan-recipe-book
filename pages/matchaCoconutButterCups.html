All Vegan Recipe Book

Plant based dishes with no animal byproducts and drinks for all occassions.

Ingredients

---Almond Butter Filling---

3 tbsp maple syrup

¾ tsp coconut oil

¼ cup + 2 tbsp creamy and smooth roasted almond butter

¼ tsp salt

---Matcha Coconut Butter Shell---

215g unsweetened shredded coconut

1 tbsp coconut oil, melted

1½ tsp sugar (or sub coconut sugar)

¾ tsp matcha

¾ tsp pure vanilla extract

¼ tsp salt

3 tablespoons chopped toasted almonds, for garnish

Instructions

1. Line a muffin tray with 16 paper cases.

2. Prepare the almond butter filling. Combine the maple syrup in a small bowl with the coconut oil and microwave together for 20-30 seconds, until it begins to bubble. Pour the warmed maple syrup into a small bowl with the almond butter and mix until creamy and smooth. Transfer to the freezer for 10-15 minutes to firm up while you make the shell.

3. Make the coconut butter. Place the shredded coconut in a high-powered blender or food processor and blend on high for several minutes until it turns into a thin creamy butter that drips off a spoon.

4. Add melted coconut oil, sugar, matcha, vanilla extract, and salt and blend until completely combined. The mixture should be creamy and pourable.

5. Spoon 1 teaspoon of matcha coconut butter into a lined mini muffin tin. Scoop 1 teaspoon of frozen creamy almond butter filling and flatten into a disc into the center of the coconut butter. Spoon 1 teaspoon of matcha coconut butter over the top, adding more to smooth out the top, as needed.

6. Sprinkle with chopped toasted almonds and place in the freezer for 10-15 minutes until just firm. Note, you can also place them in the fridge to firm but, but because they'll firm up slower, you'll end up with ripples in the top. Remove from freezer, and store in the fridge for up to a week.

The meal contains ingredients derived from plants, including fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, oils, whole grains, legumes, and beans. It does not contain animal by products animal products, including red meat, poultry, fish, eggs, honey and dairy products such as whey.

All recipes that show multiple items are meals that make 2 servings and single item recipes including drinks create 1 serving.

It's based off of two things, how filling the item is and how long it takes to prepare. Lunch contains moderately filling items that take less then 2hrs to prepare. Dinner contains very filling items which are protien heavy and dishes that take 2hrs or more to prepare (ex: holiday dishes).
